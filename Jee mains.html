JEE Mains

There are 30 NITs and many GIFT colleges all over India which accepts JEE Mains result. The paper contains 120 questions which are divided into three parts.

Part 1- consists of 30 mathematics questions

Part 2- consists of 30 Physics questions

Part 3- consists of 30 Chemistry questions

The pattern of the paper is multiple choice question type. Each question has four options out of which only one option will be correct. Four marks are awarded for correct answer and -1 for wrong answer. If the question is left unanswered no marks is awarded. Thus the maximum mark of the paper is 360. JEE Main has the speciality that none of the question is repeated till now, each and every question is unique. It tries to check out the concept of the candidates. Every question is composed by combining two or three concept which makes it difficult to score marks. Cut off marks is generally around 120 for general citizen, 75 for OBC and 50 for SC/ST. But it depends on the level of paper as in 2015 it got reduced to 105 for general and accordingly
